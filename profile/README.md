## ✦ &nbsp; The system was never designed.

</div>

It accreted. Forty years of acquisitions, regulations, vendor lock‑in, and 3 a.m. patches stacked into something that *technically* moves money and records — until it doesn't. The result is the most expensive healthcare apparatus on earth, and one of the most fragile.

## ✦ &nbsp; The price of fragility

</div>

<br/>

<div align="center">

|  |  |
|---|---|
| <h1 align="center"><code>$9.77M</code></h1> | **Average cost of a healthcare data breach.** Highest of any industry — and has been for **14 consecutive years**. <sub>— IBM *Cost of a Data Breach Report*</sub> |
| <h1 align="center"><code>$408</code></h1> | **Cost per breached patient record.** Roughly **3× the cross‑industry average**. Each leaked chart is a line item, a lawsuit, and a regulator. |
| <h1 align="center"><code>277 days</code></h1> | **Mean time to identify and contain a healthcare breach.** Nine months of dwell time inside systems holding the most sensitive data a person owns. |
| <h1 align="center"><code>~190M</code></h1> | **Americans exposed in a single 2024 incident** when one upstream clearinghouse went down. **Roughly one in two people in the country.** One vendor. One blast radius. |
| <h1 align="center"><code>$2.87B</code></h1> | **Direct cost of that single outage** to its parent company in the first year — before settlements, before fines, before the next one. |
| <h1 align="center"><code>725+</code></h1> | **Major healthcare breaches reported to OCR in 2023 alone.** Roughly **two new ones every day**. The baseline isn't *if*. It's *which Tuesday*. |

</div>

<br/>

> **The sector spends more defending the perimeter than most countries spend on their militaries —**
> and still loses, on average, *two breaches a day, every day, all year.*
